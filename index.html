Self-publishing means you take charge of the entire publishing process — from writing and editing to formatting, design, and distribution. You decide what your book looks like, where it gets published, and how it’s marketed. Many authors do it all on their own, while others choose to get help for specific steps. At Ingram Publishing Pro, we’re here to support you only where you ask. It identifies your book as a commercial product. You can purchase one yourself through official channels, or we can help you secure one when needed. Either way, ownership of the ISBN stays with you.

You can publish your book anywhere — that’s the beauty of self-publishing. Popular platforms include Amazon Kindle Direct Publishing (KDP), Ingram Spark (for global print distribution), Apple Books, Kobo, Barnes & Noble Press, and more. You’re free to choose one or multiple platforms. Need guidance setting them up? We’ve got you covered — only if you need us.

Marketing starts with knowing your audience. From there, you can create your own website, build a social media presence, run Amazon ads, get book reviews, host giveaways, or even start an email list. Many self-published authors wear the marketing hat themselves, while others bring in a specialist to help. Our team can help you create a custom plan if you'd like an assist — but the vision remains yours.

Self-publishing costs can range widely. If you handle everything yourself, your expenses may be minimal. But if you choose to hire experts for editing, design, or distribution help, costs can rise to $500–$5,000 for a polished, professional book. The best part? You decide where to invest. There are no hidden fees or forced packages — only what you choose to use.
